The engines

Three ways to read the road ahead.

Every forecast is a probability cone — a median path with 68% and 90% bands — never a fake promise of certainty.

Apollo

Analytic engine

Drift and volatility computed straight from price history, drawn as a closed-form cone. Instant, deterministic, and free on every plan.

InstantFree

Artemis

Monte Carlo × news

5,000 simulated price paths per run, with the daily drift tilted by live news sentiment. When the headlines turn, the cone turns with them.

Ensemble

FAQ

Questions you might have.

Is the AI agent safe?

Yes. The agent reads market data and your prompts to generate analysis — it never executes trades, moves money, or places orders. Order tickets are handed to your own broker, and you review every one.

What exactly am I downloading?

A small native macOS app (~450 KB). Unzip it, right-click → Open the first time (it's unsigned), and the dashboard appears in its own window. It uses Python 3.9+, which ships with modern macOS.

How are the three engines different?

Apollo computes the cone analytically from drift and volatility — instant and free. Artemis simulates 5,000 Monte Carlo paths and tilts the drift with live news sentiment. Perseverance (Elite) blends both into one consensus cone.

Can I shape what FAAM shows me?

That's the whole point. On first run the assistant asks you to rate twelve themes — music, tech, movies, factories and more — and seeds your watchlist from the answers. You can re-rate everything later in Settings → Portfolio focus.

Is this financial advice?

No. Forecasts are probability cones, not promises, and the agent is an information tool. You make the decisions — never trade real money on its output alone.

What about Windows or Linux?

macOS, Windows, and Linux are all ready to download today — pick your platform above. The Windows and Linux packages run locally and open in your browser. Since FAAM is pure Python, you can also run it anywhere with python3 app.py.
